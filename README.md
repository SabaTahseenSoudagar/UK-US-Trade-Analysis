# US‑UK Trade Analysis (2013–2023)
### MSc Data Science dissertation – University of Glasgow

## What this project does

Analyses $146 billion of US‑UK trade data to identify which economic factors and major events (Brexit, COVID‑19, US elections) truly drive trade flows. Built predictive models that explain **91.8% of the variance** in trade patterns.

## Key results

- **Unemployment is the strongest predictor** of import demand (r = -0.83, p < 0.01) – stronger than GDP or political events.
- **Brexit affected trade mainly through currency depreciation** (r = 0.92, p < 0.001), helping UK exports via a weaker pound.
- **COVID‑19 caused a temporary 20% drop**, but trade recovered quickly to a record $146 billion by 2023.
- **US presidential elections had no significant direct impact** on trade volumes.

## Data & methods

**Data sources** – UN Comtrade (trade flows), World Bank (GDP, unemployment, inflation, exchange rates), US Census Bureau (validation, 90‑95% accuracy)

**Tools** – Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, SciPy, scikit‑learn), Jupyter, Google Colab
